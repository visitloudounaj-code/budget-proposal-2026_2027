Why This Matters Now

The destination marketing landscape is in the middle of its sharpest disruption in a decade. Google AI Overviews are reshaping organic discovery — DMOs nationally are reporting 20–40 percent traffic shifts as AI summaries replace traditional search results. Stakeholders are demanding economic-impact proof, not impressions. Day-tripper-to-overnight conversion is the new battleground, and the tools, tactics, and content standards needed to compete are evolving faster than published playbooks can keep up.

Every conference in this proposal addresses one of three strategic gaps:

  • How to structure VisitLoudoun.org content so AI search engines cite Loudoun as an authoritative destination — not skip past it
  • How to measure and prove marketing's contribution to overnight visitation and lodging revenue
  • How peer DMOs are solving the same challenges, what's actually working, and what to avoid

ESTO — Educational Seminar for Tourism Organizations

August 23–26, 2026 Philadelphia, PA U.S. Travel Association
Priority Tier 1 Top Recommendation

ESTO is the flagship industry conference for U.S. destination marketing organizations. It is open exclusively to destination professionals — DMO, CVB, and state tourism office staff — with no outside industry sectors permitted to attend. Every session, every roundtable, every networking conversation is with someone doing the same kind of work I do. The agenda maps directly to my job description, and Philadelphia is reachable by Amtrak for under $200 roundtrip.
